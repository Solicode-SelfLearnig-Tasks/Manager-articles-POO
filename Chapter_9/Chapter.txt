Chapter 9: Encapsulation and Best Practices

In this chapter, I learned how to protect class data and ensure its consistency through encapsulation and validation best practices.

Key things to remember:

1. Data Encapsulation:
- Use the `private` keyword for properties.
- This prevents direct access or accidental modification from outside the class.

2. Getters and Setters:
- `getX()` (Getter): Provide read access to a private property.
- `setX($value)` (Setter): Provide write access to a private property.

3. Validation and Sanitization in Setters:
- Setters are the ideal place to check data before it is stored.
- Example: `strlen($value) > 3` to ensure minimum length.
- Example: `htmlspecialchars($value)` to prevent XSS and ensure data sanitization.

4. Writing Clean Code:
- Use meaningful names for methods and variables.
- Keep methods focused on one task (Single Responsibility).

Conclusion:
Encapsulation isn't just about security; it's about building robust and reliable classes that protect their internal state!
